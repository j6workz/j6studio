## Day 1 Afternoon — Terminal 21 Asok

**Get there:** BTS Nana → Asok (2 stops). Exit BTS directly into the mall — no walking outside.
**Hours:** 10:00–22:00 daily
**Pro tip:** Pick up a Tourist Privilege Card at the information counter (Ground Floor) for discounts at participating stores. Free, takes 2 min.

Terminal 21 is themed as an airport — each floor = a different global city. Use the escalator signage to navigate (they read like departure boards).

### Floor by floor

**Ground Floor (Rome) — Arrival Hall**
- Currency exchange counter here if you need THB top-up
- Daiso — 60 THB per item Japanese variety store. Stock up on travel organizers, skincare tools, small gifts. Great value.
- Watsons — sunscreen, basic toiletries, Mistine cosmetics. Stock up if you forgot anything.
- Boots — same category as Watsons, slightly pricier, more international brands.
- Madame Heng herbal soap — Thai brand, natural glycerin soap bars 60–150 THB each. Good take-home gift.
- ChaTraMue Thai Tea counter — try the original Thai iced milk tea here. This brand is the one everyone copies.

**Floor 1 (Tokyo) — Women's fashion**
- Local Thai indie fashion brands dominate. Think flowy linen co-ords, oversized tees, Korean-style fits.
- Prices: tops 200–500 THB, sets 400–900 THB.
- **Note for Ferina:** Sizes here go up to XL–2XL at most stalls. If she needs larger, skip this floor and go to Krungthong Plaza instead. Don't waste time here if fit is the priority.
- Good for J6: basic linen shirts, cotton shorts. Thai sizing for men is generous — XL here usually fits a Malaysian large-XL.

**Floor 2 (London) — Men's fashion + sportswear**
- Decathlon — reliable sports gear at Thai prices. Running shoes, gym wear, socks. Cheaper than in MY.
- Foot Locker — sneakers, branded sportswear.
- Local menswear shops — cotton/linen shirts 200–500 THB, casual pants 300–700 THB.
- Tomato Jeans — affordable jeans, decent quality, 400–700 THB.

**Floor 3 (Istanbul) — Accessories + cosmetics**
- Korean-style accessories shops — earrings, rings, necklaces 100–300 THB.
- Eveandboy — Thai cosmetics megastore. Stock: Mistine, Snail White, SIVANNA, Karmart. All cheaper here than at airport.
  - **Mistine BB Cream** — 90–150 THB (vs RM 25–40 in KL, same or cheaper here)
  - **Snail White moisturiser** — 250–400 THB
  - **Karmart Cathy Doll serum** — 150–250 THB
- Sunscreen stalls — Bioré UV Aqua Rich 100 THB, Banana Boat SPF50 120–180 THB.

**Floor 4–5 (San Francisco + Caribbean) — Pier 21 Food Court**
- This is the main food destination. Uses a pre-paid card system.
- Get a card at the counter → load with cash (100–300 THB) → order → unused balance refunded when you leave.
- **What to order:**
  - Pad See Ew — wide flat noodles, soy sauce, egg, protein. 55–70 THB. Better than tourist restaurants.
  - Tom Kha Gai — coconut chicken soup. 60–80 THB.
  - Mango Sticky Rice — 60–80 THB, portions are generous.
  - Som Tum — green papaya salad. 50–60 THB. Tell them "mai phet" (not spicy) if needed.
  - Cha Yen (Thai iced tea) — 25–35 THB.
  - Zeed restaurant (near elevator, 4F) — internet-famous, try the crab curry yellow rice. 120–180 THB.
- **Halal note:** Most noodle dishes use pork-free broth IF you ask. Point to what you want and say "mai sai moo" (no pork). Vegetable + egg dishes are safest. Look for halal stickers on individual counters.

**What NOT to waste time on at Terminal 21:**
- The themed decorations are great for photos but don't get lost taking selfies — you have a schedule.
- Skip the cinema and bowling on Floor 5 — not on this trip.

## Day 1 Evening — Jodd Fairs Ratchada Night Market

**Get there:** MRT Sukhumvit → Thailand Cultural Centre (Exit 4). 3-min walk to market. Open 5 PM–1 AM.

Not a structured mall — it's 1,500+ open stalls. Navigate by walking the main lanes. Here's what to actually look for:

### Food (eat these, in this order)
1. **Leng Zabb** — find it near the centre of the market, look for the queue. Volcano ribs (pork — **J6 only**), or order chicken ribs (ไก่) as halal substitute — confirm with them. 180–300 THB.
2. **Make Me Mango** — stall with the large mango display. Fresh mango smoothie with actual mango chunks. 80 THB. Halal-safe.
3. **Toro Fries** — massive fried potato spirals on a stick, multiple dipping sauces. 100 THB. Halal-safe.
4. **Grilled seafood stalls** — look for live display tanks near the back of the market. Grilled fish, prawns, shellfish. Tell them "mai sai butter" if you want it plain. 150–350 THB per plate. Halal-safe if grilled only.
5. **Korean fried chicken stalls** — several stalls with halal markings (green sticker). 120–200 THB.
6. **Madam Luck** — halal Thai stall. Look for the green halal sign in the front area. Rice dishes 60–90 THB.
7. **Mango sticky rice** — sold by individual vendors throughout. 60–80 THB.

### What to buy (clothing + accessories)
- **Linen and cotton tops** — look for stalls selling "linen" (ลินิน) pieces. Flowy tops for Ferina: 150–300 THB. Check size first — most go to L/XL, some to 2XL.
- **Elephant pants** — the classic tourist buy. Comfortable, breathable, pattern-printed wide-leg pants. 100–200 THB. Multiple sizes. Actually worth buying for the KL heat too.
- **Tote bags / canvas bags** — printed Thai designs, useful, 80–150 THB.
- **Handmade earrings** — beaded, resin, shell designs. 80–200 THB per pair.
- **Beaded bracelets** — customizable, they string them on the spot. 80–150 THB.
- **Phone accessories** — cases, grips, charms. 50–150 THB.
- **Souvenirs** — fridge magnets, keychain packs, mini Buddha figurines. 50–150 THB.
- **100 THB shop** — look for signs saying ทุกอย่าง 100 (everything 100 THB). Mixed clothing and accessories at flat rate. Worth a 10-min rummage.

### Bargaining at Jodd Fairs
- Clothing stalls: start at 70% of asking price. They rarely go below 60%. Smile.
- After 9:30 PM closing vendors are more flexible — last 30 min before closing is best for deals.
- Food stalls: never bargain.
- Electronics or phone accessories: you can try but margins are thin.

## Day 2 — Krungthong Plaza (Ferina's plus-size priority stop)

**Get there:** BTS Nana → Phrom Phong → Grab (or BTS to Chidlom → 8 min walk). OR from Wat Pho area: Grab direct, ~25–30 min, 150 THB.
**Hours:** Opens ~9 AM (some stalls from 8 AM), most close by 6–7 PM. **Go before 5 PM.**
**Location:** 77/1 Phetburi Rd, Ratchathewi (Pratunam area, next to Platinum Mall).

4 floors, 600+ shops, sizes S–8XL. This is Bangkok's dedicated plus-size market. No other mall in Bangkok comes close for Ferina's sizing needs.

### Critical tips before entering
- **No proper fitting rooms** in most stalls. Wear thin, stretchy clothing you can try things over. Leggings + thin top is ideal.
- **Staff can size you by eye** — if you stand at a stall and look uncertain, they'll pull the right size out immediately. Let them help.
- **Wholesale pricing kicks in at 2–3 pieces** from the same stall. Example: one piece 500 THB → two pieces 450 THB each. Always ask "tho rai song chin?" (how much for two?).
- Bring cash only. Most stalls don't accept cards. ATM available inside the mall.
- Carry a measuring tape if you have one, or know your bust/waist/hip measurements in inches.

### Floor by floor

**Floor 1 — Casual everyday tops, T-shirts, basic separates**
- Most affordable floor. Tops from 150–250 THB.
- Look for linen-blend and cotton fabric. Ask "sai lin-in mai?" (is this linen?).
- Good for basics — plain tees, flowy blouses, casual sets.
- Check sizing stickers: Thai sizing here goes in Thai numbers. 44–52 inch bust size is standard stock.

**Floor 2 — Mixed fashion: tops, pants, dresses, accessories + bra specialty**
- **Muenfun Shop (Floor 2)** — spicy/trendy plus-size fashion. Bodycon dresses, bold prints. Bust 44–48 inches.
- **Fat Boys (Floor 2, Room 2027–2029)** — men's plus-size. Cotton shirts, Hawaii prints, shorts. Men's chest 42"–70". English-speaking staff. **This is J6's stop if Uniqlo doesn't work out.**
  - Hawaii shirts: 350–550 THB
  - Plain cotton shirts (long/short): 300–500 THB
  - Shorts/pants: 300–600 THB
- **No's Bra (Floor 2)** — plus-size bras, shapewear, seamless underwear. Western sizes available. Very hard to find elsewhere in Bangkok. If Ferina needs this, this is the only reliable spot.
- Pants and wide-leg trousers: 300–500 THB
- Printed linen sets (top + pants): 500–900 THB

**Floor 3 — Dresses, evening wear, kaftans**
- **Baifern Shop (Floor 3)** — cocktail and evening dresses, special occasion. Chest 40"–48", waist 36"–42". Bold colors, flattering cuts. 500–1,200 THB.
- Kaftan stalls near food court — loose, breathable, comfortable travel dresses. 300–600 THB. Ferina can wear these in KL too. Halal-modest friendly.
- Maxi dresses in batik and printed fabric: 350–600 THB.

**Floor 4 — Trendy fashion, sweet/chic styles**
- **Netnechaar Shop (Floor 4)** — cute and chic plus-size fashion. Sizes 32"–42" (smaller than lower floors). Dresses, trendy tops, stylish pants. 300–700 THB.
- **Vanilla (Floor 4)** — popular chain, well-reviewed by Wanderlog visitors. Comfortable casual-smart pieces.
- **Kays Closet** — look for this stall name, good variety reviews.
- **Sugar2** — trendy pieces reviewed well by regular shoppers.
- **Pleated fashion** — pleated skirts, blouses. Very on-trend. 300–500 THB.

### What to specifically look for for Ferina
- Linen co-ord sets (top + pants) — breathable for KL heat after the trip. 500–900 THB.
- Printed kaftan dresses — comfortable for travel + daily use. 300–600 THB.
- Wide-leg palazzo pants — easy to pair, flattering on most body types. 300–500 THB.
- Flowy blouses — can dress up or down. 200–400 THB.
- Batik-print pieces — authentic regional fabric patterns. 350–700 THB.

### Halal note
No food stalls relevant here. There is a small food court for a budget break — standard Thai fare, ask before eating.

## Day 2 Evening — ICONSIAM + SookSiam

**Get there:** BTS Nana → Saphan Taksin → Sathorn Pier → free shuttle boat (every 10 min, 5-min ride).
**Note:** Shuttle boats were operated free by ICONSIAM but as of May 2023 may charge a small fee (~10–15 THB). Verify at the pier. Chao Phraya Tourist Boat (Blue Flag) also stops here if shuttle unavailable.
**Hours:** Mall 10:00–22:00. SookSiam same hours. Fountain show: 7 PM, 8 PM, 9 PM.

### Floor guide

**G Floor — SookSiam (entire ground floor back section)**
This is a 15,000 sqm indoor recreation of Thailand's 77 provinces. Walk the full perimeter before buying anything — do one loop first, decide what you want, then come back to purchase.

*Divided into 4 regional zones: North, Northeast, Central, South*

**Food to eat at SookSiam (halal-safe confirmed):**
- **Pad Thai Thipsamai** — the legendary 1939 restaurant now has a branch here. Halal certified. Egg-wrapped Pad Thai (Pad Thai ho khai) 80–120 THB. The egg-wrapped version is the signature — order this.
- **Southern Thai** — the southern zone has halal food. Look for green halal signs (ฮาลาล).
- **Khao Yam** (southern rice salad) — light, herbal, halal-safe. 60–80 THB.
- **Rotee** — Thai-Muslim flatbread with sweet dipping sauce. 40–60 THB.
- **Mango sticky rice** — available throughout. 60–80 THB.
- **Fresh coconut water** — served in the shell. 50–70 THB.
- **Thai tea/coffee** — hand-labeled milk tea, strong and sweet. 40–60 THB.
- **Northern Thai sai oua sausage** — herbal pork sausage. J6 only. 80–120 THB.

**What to buy at SookSiam:**
- **Thai silk scarves** — North zone. Handwoven, multiple colour options. 250–800 THB. These are the real thing, not tourist market replicas.
- **Handwoven cotton fabric** — Northeast (Isaan) zone. Mudmee silk-cotton patterns, distinctive. 300–1,200 THB/metre.
- **Benjarong ceramic ware** — small decorative plates, cups. Central zone. 300–1,500 THB. Genuine royal Thai porcelain style.
- **Herbal products** — Prai cream (anti-inflammatory), ya dom (herbal inhaler), lemongrass balm. 80–300 THB. Better quality here than at market stalls.
- **Thai coconut sugar** — dense, caramel-like. 80–150 THB per pack. Great cooking ingredient to bring home.
- **Nam prik (chilli paste) packs** — vacuum sealed, safe to fly. 60–120 THB. Authentic regional varieties.
- **Kratip rattan boxes** — small woven rattan containers used for sticky rice. Beautiful gift item. 100–300 THB.
- **Hand-painted ceramics** — small bowls, cups, decorative pieces by regional artisans. 200–600 THB.
- **Thai spa products (Love Massage Rak Herbal)** — traditional herbal compress products, massage oil. 150–500 THB.

**Floor 1 — ICONLUXE luxury zone + H&M, Uniqlo, Zara**
- H&M — standard H&M, similar prices to KL. Skip unless you see a sale.
- **Uniqlo (Floor 1)** — Thai pricing is similar to MY but some items are cheaper. AIRism T-shirts 390 THB, linen shirts 790 THB. Sizes up to 3XL men / 3XL women.
- Zara — standard Zara, prices similar to MY.

**Floor 2 — Apple Store (Thailand's first official Apple store)**
- iPad, iPhone, AirPods — same Apple pricing globally, no discount. But if anything breaks or you need genuine accessories, this is the most reliable source.
- Apple Thailand pricing note: iPhones can be marginally cheaper than Malaysia depending on tax. Check current prices before assuming.

**Floor 4 — Siam Takashimaya (Japanese department store)**
- Cosmetics counters on this floor — SHISEIDO, SK-II, Sulwhasoo, Lancôme. Similar pricing to MY duty-free. Not significantly cheaper.
- Japanese skincare brands — some Japan-only products available here that you can't find in KL. Worth a browse if you're into J-beauty.
- Japanese supermarket section (Basement of Takashimaya) — Japanese snacks, instant noodles, pantry items. Good for gifts.

**Floor 4 (ICONSIAM main) — ICONCRAFT**
- Thai designer crafts, handmade goods, contemporary artisanal products.
- Hand-painted canvas bags: 350–700 THB
- Locally designed jewellery: 300–1,200 THB
- Thai ceramic mugs and bowls: 200–500 THB
- Scented candles with Thai botanicals: 300–800 THB

**THANN (multiple floors)** — Premium Thai spa brand. Aromatic wood body wash, hand cream, room spray. Very popular gift. 300–1,200 THB. The Aromatic Wood shower gel is the most loved product. Widely available but ICONSIAM has full selection.

**Karmakamet (Floor 2–3 area)** — Luxury Thai home fragrance and wellness brand. Scented sachets, diffusers, body oil. 300–1,500 THB. Distinctive black packaging. Makes excellent premium gifts.

**Riverfront Terrace (G Floor, outdoor)** — After shopping, come here 10 min before the 7 PM fountain show. The terraced riverside seating fills up fast. Stand near the river edge for best view. Show is 15–20 min. No need to book, just show up early.

## Day 3 Morning — Chatuchak Weekend Market (JJ Market)

**Get there:** BTS Mo Chit (Exit 1) or MRT Kamphaeng Phet (Exit 2 — slightly better, drops you right at Zone 2 entrance). Walk 3–5 min following the crowd.
**Hours:** Sat–Sun 9 AM–6 PM. **Your window: 9:00–11:30 AM sharp.**
**First thing on arrival:** Photograph the large section map at the main entrance near Gate 2. This is your navigation tool for the entire visit.
**Yellow signs with red lettering** = section numbers. Use these to navigate.
**ATM:** Bangkok Bank ATM at Gate 1 near the main entrance. Withdraw before entering.

The market is divided into 27 sections. Below is your targeted route for 2.5 hours.

### Suggested route (start at MRT Kamphaeng Phet Exit 2 → Zone 2 → work your way through)

**Sections 2–4 — Women's fashion (start here)**
- This is the highest density of trendy, independent fashion at Chatuchak.
- Predominantly local Thai designer labels — pieces you won't find in KL.
- **What to look for for Ferina:**
  - Linen co-ord sets: 300–600 THB
  - Boho/flowy blouses: 200–400 THB
  - Wide-leg linen pants: 250–450 THB
  - Vintage-style dresses: 200–500 THB
  - **Important:** Sizes here are mostly S–XL. Very limited 2XL+. If stall doesn't have her size, move on — don't spend time asking them to check.
- Zone 2 has hip boutique-style shops started by young Thai designers. Quality is better here than Jodd Fairs.
- Graphic tees with Thai script/art: 150–300 THB. Cool unique pieces.
- Second-hand vintage clothing: look for stalls with mixed racks. 100–400 THB. Japanese tourists come specifically for these.

**Sections 5–6 — Shoes, bags, accessories**
- Sandals: leather-look flats, woven sandals 200–500 THB. Thai sizing is small — try before buying.
- Bags: canvas totes 150–300 THB, woven rattan bags 300–700 THB, faux leather crossbody 300–600 THB.
- Woven straw hats: 100–250 THB. Functional in the Bangkok heat.
- Hair accessories: 50–200 THB. Claw clips, headbands, scrunchies — Ferina can stock up.

**Section 7 — Art, paintings, handmade prints (unique stop)**
- This is Chatuchak's open-air gallery. Entirely original work by Thai artists.
- In 2026: look for neo-traditionalist Thai paintings (Buddhist motifs in pop-art colour), hand-painted ceramics, and graphic prints.
- Small original prints: 200–600 THB. Wall-art quality.
- Custom pet portrait (on the spot or commission): 390–700 THB. If you want one of Jessy/Roti Bakar or the gang, show them a clear photo — they start drawing immediately.
- Watercolour prints of Bangkok temples: 300–800 THB. Good souvenir.
- Hand-painted wooden signs: 200–500 THB.

**Section 1 — Antiques, Buddhist amulets, collectibles**
- Genuine antique dealers and Buddha amulet stalls.
- Thai Buddha amulets: 100–2,000+ THB (price = believed power + rarity). Locals wear these daily.
- Vintage Thai items, old coins, lacquerware: 200–5,000 THB.
- Note: Exporting genuine antiques and certain Buddha images from Thailand requires permits. Small amulets and replica pieces are fine.

**Sections 8–11 — Handicrafts, woodwork, Thai souvenirs**
- Hand-carved wooden elephant figurines: 100–500 THB.
- Lacquerware bowls and trays: 200–800 THB.
- Woven rattan baskets and storage: 200–700 THB.
- Thai-style lanterns (folding paper): 100–300 THB. Pack flat.
- Cushion covers in Thai fabric: 200–500 THB.
- Traditional Thai herbal soap: 80–200 THB.

**Sections 11, 13, 15, 17, 19, 25 — Ceramics**
- Handmade pottery: cups, bowls, plates. 100–500 THB.
- Celadon-style (jade green glaze) — distinctive Thai style: 200–600 THB.
- Good for gifts — pack wrapped in clothing in your checked bag.

**Section 16 / Soi 24 — Saman Islam (HALAL FOOD STOP)**
- This is your meal during Chatuchak. Eat here at 10:30 AM before the worst heat.
- **What's on offer:**
  - Beef biryani: 80–120 THB
  - Beef noodle soup (clear or thick broth): 70–100 THB
  - Halal Pad Thai: 60–80 THB
  - Murtabak (stuffed flatbread): 60–90 THB
  - Thai Muslim curry rice: 70–100 THB
- Look for the halal signs (ฮาลาล or الحلال). This whole zone is halal-dedicated.
- MIXT Chatuchak mall is right next to the market — has a halal food court inside with prayer room. Use as backup if Section 16 is crowded.

**Section 26 — Handmade jewellery**
- Silver rings: 200–500 THB
- Resin/epoxy earrings — flowers, patterns encased in clear resin. Very popular Thai export. 150–350 THB per pair.
- Beaded bracelets: made on the spot, 80–200 THB.
- Wire-wrapped stone pendants: 150–400 THB.
- Semi-precious stone accessories: 200–800 THB.

**Section 27 — Antiques + vintage**
- Same category as Section 1 but more focused on vintage fashion and furniture.
- Vintage band tees (J6): 200–500 THB. Some genuine vintage, some prints.
- Old cameras, radios, vintage toys: collectors' items. 500–5,000 THB.

**The coconut ice cream** — sold throughout the market. Look for stalls with young coconuts displayed, scooping ice cream from a metal container directly into the coconut shell. 70 THB. Halal. Get this. It is one of the genuinely unmissable Bangkok street foods. You can add toppings (corn, taro, pandan jelly) for +10–20 THB.

**Chatuchak bargaining rules:**
- Start at 60–70% of asking price (NOT 50% — that's too aggressive and will offend).
- Buy 2+ from the same stall = leverage for 10–20% off.
- Smile throughout. The moment you look annoyed the price goes up.
- Never bargain at food stalls — that's disrespectful.
- If they say a firm no: start walking away slowly. 60% of the time they'll call you back.
- First sale of the day (9–9:30 AM) = vendor wants good luck, more likely to deal.
- Taking a photo of the stall number (displayed on each unit) is how you find it again. You will absolutely get lost. This is fine.

## Day 3 Afternoon — Siam Paragon

**Get there:** BTS Siam (Exit 3–5). Paragon is directly connected to the BTS exit via covered walkway.
**Hours:** 10:00–22:00. **Your window: 12:00–13:50 PM. Leave by 13:50 sharp.**
**Tourist tip:** Get a Paragon Tourist Privilege Card at the information counter near the main entrance (Floor M or G). Shows passport → gets you additional discounts at select shops. Takes 5 min and can save you 5–10% at some stores.

This is the best place in Bangkok to buy take-home food gifts. Air-conditioned, well-organized, quality packaging.

- **Otop snacks** — coconut wafer rolls (look for the green/gold tin packaging), pandan cookies, Thai crackers. 80–200 THB per pack.
- **Kanom Krok mix** — Thai coconut pancake mix. 80–150 THB.
- **Premium Thai coffee** — Doi Chaang, Akha Ama brands. Real single-origin Thai mountain coffee. 200–500 THB per 200g bag. Best coffee gift from Thailand.

**Floor G — Main concourse + Boots + Watsons**

**Floor 3 (3F) — Naraya + Jim Thompson + home décor**

## Day 2/3 Storm Alt — Platinum Fashion Mall (Pratunam)

**Get there:** BTS Chidlom → 10 min walk, OR BTS Ratchathewi → 10 min walk. Connected by skywalk to Pratunam Market across the road.
**Hours:** 09:00–20:00 daily. Wholesale buyers crowd Wed/Thu.
**Why it's the storm alt:** 5 floors fully AC, 2,000+ stalls, biggest fashion wholesale mall in SE Asia. Survives any storm.

### Floor by floor

**G–F2 — Mainstream women's fashion (regular sizing S–L)**
- Standard Thai fashion wholesale. Skip if Ferina priority is fit.
- Tops 150–350 THB, dresses 200–500, skirts 200–400.

**Floor 3 — Plus-size women (this is the floor for Ferina)**
- Sizes 2XL–6XL across most stalls.
- Linen sets 350–700 THB. Wide-leg trousers 250–450.
- Cotton blouses 200–400. Maxi dresses 350–600.
- Wholesale rule: 3 pieces same shop = 10–20% off. Always ask "tho rai sam chin?"

**Floor 4 — Plus-size + men's plus-size + accessories**

## 7-Eleven Essentials — Things Only Thailand Has

7-Eleven (สเว่นอีเลฟเว่น) is on every block. Stock these unique items at any of the dozens you'll pass:

### Snacks worth buying
- **Toasties (ham/cheese/sausage croissant pressed warm)** — 30–45 THB. Hot 24/7. Better than airport food.
- **Lays Nori Seaweed flavour** — 25 THB, unique Thai-only flavour. Stack 5 packs for home.
- **Lays Sour Cream + Onion** — Thai recipe slightly different from MY.
- **Pringles Seaweed / Salted Egg / Cheese Soft Shell Crab** — Thai-exclusive flavours, 80–110 THB per can.
- **Kit Kat Matcha / Strawberry / Cookies & Cream** — Thai variants 35–50 THB.
- **Pocky Chocolate-Banana / Almond Crush** — 30–45 THB.
- **Hi-Chew Mango / Lychee / Pineapple** — 35 THB.

### Drinks worth trying
- **Cha Tra Mue Original Thai Tea** (red/orange bottle) — 25 THB. The brand that defines Thai tea.
- **est Cola** — Thai version, slightly less sweet. 15 THB. Try once.
- **Jub Jang Thai herbal water** — herbal infusion with longan/chrysanthemum. 20–35 THB.
- **Coconut water in fresh shell** (some 7-11s) — 60–80 THB.
- **Yakult Thailand** — different probiotic strain than MY version. 30 THB pack.

### Useful daily items
- **Sunscreen (Bioré UV Aqua Rich, Anessa) ** — 280–450 THB. Same/cheaper than MY.
- **Wet wipes / hand sanitiser** — 25–60 THB.
- **Hot food bento boxes** — they microwave on the spot. Pad thai, fried rice, chicken rice 40–70 THB. Halal-marked options available.
- **Ya dom (herbal inhaler)** — 30–50 THB. Get one per person, use it on the BTS, in temples, anywhere stuffy.
- **Plug adapters (Type A/C universal)** — 80–150 THB. Less dodgy than airport ones.

### What NOT to buy at 7-Eleven
- **SIM cards** — buy at airport on arrival (DTAC/AIS tourist SIMs). 7-11 cards work but airport bundles are better value.
- **Alcohol after 14:00–17:00** — Thai law bans sale 14:00–17:00 daily. Confusing but real.

## 🚫 What NOT to buy / Avoid

- **"Genuine" luxury goods at markets** — Gucci, LV, Rolex at markets are all fake. Buying counterfeit goods is technically illegal.
- **Gems and jewellery from tuk-tuk tours** — classic scam. If a driver takes you to a "special gem store", everything there is overpriced junk.
- **Silk at roadside tourist stalls near temples** — low quality synthetic fabric sold as silk. Buy from SookSiam, Jim Thompson, or Chatuchak Section 7.
- **Herbal inhalers (ya dom)** from street vendors — quality varies wildly. Buy from Boots/Watsons for consistent quality.
- **Everything at the airport** — same items 30–50% more expensive than in-city. Only buy there if you completely forget something.
