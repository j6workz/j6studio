🛏 Check in — lyf Sukhumvit 8

🏬 Terminal 21 Asok — first taste of Bangkok shopping
10 AM–10 PM

Take BTS from Nana station (5-min walk from hotel) two stops to Asok BTS station. Terminal 21 is connected directly to the station — no outdoor walking needed, full air-con the moment you step in. Perfect for the hottest part of the day.

Each floor is themed as a different world city: Rome (G floor), Tokyo (2F), London (3F), Istanbul (4F), San Francisco (5F). The Pier 21 Food Court on the 5th floor basement (or top floor depending on the branch) serves authentic Thai food at local prices — Pad Thai from 50 THB.

What to buy here

  • Local fashion brands — reasonably priced, good for casual wear
  • Accessories & bags — local brands, 200–500 THB
  • Phone accessories & gadgets — various floors

3:30 PM
✂️ Grooming — MOGA Hair Salon (GF) + Barber (J6)
GF: ~1,500–2,500 THB · J6: ~200 THB

For her — MOGA Hair Salon, Asoke Tower (Sukhumvit 21): Walk or short Grab from Terminal 21 (both are at the Asok junction). MOGA is a 30+ year Japanese-style salon that explicitly specialises in thinning hair treatments using Aveda products. Their stylists do a full consultation first — face shape, lifestyle, hair condition — before touching scissors. Let the stylist recommend what suits her best.

Book in advance: +66 2 xxx xxxx (call or Line them). Ask for the consultation package that covers cut + treatment for fine/thinning hair.

For J6 — Barber near Nana/Asok: Dozens of men's grooming shops on Sukhumvit Soi 3–8. Ask hotel front desk for the nearest one. Beard trim typically 150–250 THB. Takes 20–30 min. Book a separate appointment or walk in while she's at MOGA.

Duration: Allow 1.5–2 hours for her hair. You can finish your beard trim and meet her after.

🚶 Walk or short Grab Terminal 21 → Asoke Tower (MOGA) → ~5–10 min Walk or ~50 THB Grab
Book ahead: MOGA fills up, especially on weekdays when locals come. Book via Line app a day or two before. Ask specifically for a stylist experienced with fine or thinning hair. MOGA's consultation is thorough — she'll leave with a style that actually works for her hair type.
5:45 PM
🌙 Jodd Fairs Ratchada — Bangkok's best daily night market
Free entryOpen daily 5 PM–1 AM

From Asok, take the MRT Blue Line from Sukhumvit MRT station (underground, directly below Asok BTS) to Thailand Cultural Centre station — 2 stops, ~6 min. Take Exit 4 and walk 3 minutes to the market entrance.

Over 1,500 stalls — food, fashion, accessories, live music from 7–8 PM onwards. The atmosphere is lively and the crowd is young and local. Arrive by 6 PM for best food selection before it fills up.

Must-try food: Leng Zabb volcano ribs (180 THB), grilled rainbow shellfish platters, mango sticky rice, fresh mango smoothies, pork skewers (moo ping) from 20 THB each. Korean fried chicken from the back section.

What to buy at Jodd Fairs

  • Accessories: earrings, necklaces, rings — 50–200 THB
  • Handbags & tote bags — 150–400 THB
  • Yadom (nasal inhalers) — 30–50 THB, cheaper here than pharmacies
  • Thai souvenirs: magnets, keychains, elephant figurines — 40–150 THB
  • Quirky phone cases & accessories
🚇 MRT Blue Line Sukhumvit MRT → Thailand Cultural Centre (Exit 4) · 2 stops · ~6 min ~20 THB each way
Bargaining: Start at 60–70% of asking price. Smile, be friendly. Buy multiple items from one stall for better discount. Never haggle at food stalls — prices are fixed.

Day Two

Temples. River. ICONSIAM.

Friday, 1 May — Labour Day
35°C 25% rain chance — best weather day Temples open as normal on Labour Day. Malls open. Hit the Grand Palace at opening time — non-negotiable.
7:30 AM
☕ Breakfast — fuel up before temples
50–120 THB

Eat at the hotel or walk to 7-Eleven nearby for quick bites (Thai onigiri, bread, milk). Or find a street breakfast stall — look for Khanom Krok (coconut rice pancakes) or Jok (rice porridge), both under 60 THB.

8:15 AM
⛵ Head to Grand Palace — scenic river route

Take BTS from Nana to Saphan Taksin (end of Silom Line, ~6 stops, ~20 min). Exit at Saphan Taksin → walk to Sathorn Pier (Central Pier) — it's right there. Board the Chao Phraya Express Boat (orange flag) heading north to Tha Chang Pier (N9). Ride takes ~25 min.

This river route is spectacular — you'll pass the skyline, temples, and get your first views of Wat Arun from the water. Much better than fighting traffic by taxi.

🚈 BTS + Chao Phraya Boat Nana BTS → Saphan Taksin BTS → Sathorn Pier → Tha Chang Pier (N9) BTS ~40 THB + Boat ~16 THB each · ~45 min total
Important: Only board the orange-flag boats (Chao Phraya Express Boat). Tourist boats charge 200 THB but the local orange flag boat is the same trip for 16 THB.
9:00 AM
🏛 Grand Palace + Wat Phra Kaew (Emerald Buddha)
8:30 AM – 3:30 PM500 THB/person

Walk 2 min from Tha Chang pier to the main gate. Tickets are 500 THB per person (cash or card at kiosk). Dress code is strict — both of you need covered shoulders and knees. Sarongs and cover-up skirts are available to borrow/rent at the gate.

Spend 1.5–2 hours here. The highlights:

• Wat Phra Kaew (Emerald Buddha temple) — the most sacred site. Remove shoes at the entrance. No photography inside. The jade Buddha statue is small but the gilded hall is extraordinary.
• Chakri Maha Prasat Hall — the fusion European-Thai architecture.
• Outer walls murals — 178 panels depicting the Ramakien epic. Walk the full length.
• The golden Phra Si Rattana Chedi spire — unmissable for photos.

Carry your water. There are some shaded areas but the courtyard is largely exposed. Take breaks in the shade. Don't rush — this is the most important cultural site in Thailand.

Scam alert: If anyone outside the gate tells you it's closed today and offers to take you elsewhere — ignore them. The palace is open. Walk to the ticket window yourself.
11:00 AM
🙏 Wat Pho — Reclining Buddha + Thai Massage
8 AM – 7:30 PM300 THB entry · massage from 300 THB

5-minute walk south from the Grand Palace (just follow signs or crowds). Entry: 300 THB (includes a small bottle of water at the gate). Allow 1 hour to 1.5 hours.

The Reclining Buddha — 46m long, gold leaf, 15m high — is genuinely breathtaking in person. Walk around it fully. At the feet, you'll find 108 bronze bowls — drop a coin in each for good luck (bring small change).

Wat Pho is the birthplace of traditional Thai massage and home to the original school. Get a massage here — it's authentic, affordable, and your girlfriend will appreciate the pampering after a morning of walking in heat.

• Traditional Thai Massage: 30 min 300 THB · 1 hr 420 THB
• Foot massage: 30 min 300 THB
• No booking needed — walk into the massage building on the temple grounds.

🚶 Walk Grand Palace exit → Wat Pho entrance · ~5 min, 600m Free
For her: Request the foot massage + head/neck extension — wonderful for tense shoulders. Tell the therapist your pain/pressure preference before they start.
12:30 PM
⛩️ Wat Arun — Temple of Dawn
8 AM – 6 PM100 THB entry

Walk 10 minutes south from Wat Pho to Tha Tien Pier. The cross-river ferry is just 4 THB per person — runs constantly, 5-minute ride. You can see Wat Arun's iconic prang (spire) from the pier — stunning composition for photos from the boat.

Entry is 100 THB. Spend 45–60 minutes here. The porcelain-covered prang is the most intricate thing you'll see in Bangkok — every square inch is decorated with smashed Chinese porcelain in elaborate patterns. You can climb partway up the central tower for river views.

After Wat Arun, sit at one of the riverside cafes or restaurants with a cold drink and river view — you've earned it. This is the hottest part of the day. Don't rush back.

⛵ Cross-river ferry Tha Tien Pier → Wat Arun pier · ~3 min 4 THB each way per person

5:00 PM
🌊 ICONSIAM — riverside mega-mall + fountain show
Free entry10 AM – 10 PM

Take BTS from Nana → Saphan Taksin (same route as morning). At Sathorn Pier, take the free ICONSIAM shuttle boat — departs every 10 minutes, 5-minute ride. Walk right for ICONSIAM boat (walk left for Asiatique).

What to see & do inside:

• SookSiam (Ground floor) — The indoor floating market recreating all 77 provinces of Thailand. Street food from 30–80 THB, Thai handicrafts, regional snacks, live cultural performances. This is the highlight of ICONSIAM — allow 45 minutes here alone.
• ICONLUXE (1F) — Gucci, LV, Dior, Prada if browsing. Don't need to buy anything.
• Apple Store — Thailand's first Apple Store, on the river terrace.
• River terrace (6F) — panoramic views of Chao Phraya. Great for sunset photos.
• Free fountain show — riverside, runs at 7:00 PM, 8:00 PM, and 9:00 PM. The 7 PM show is the one to catch — 400m of multimedia fountain jets. Spectacular and completely free.

Have dinner in SookSiam food zone (budget-friendly) or one of the mid-range restaurants on upper floors.

What to buy at ICONSIAM
  • SookSiam — regional Thai products, snacks, herbal products, silk items
  • ICONCRAFT (4F–5F) — quality Thai handicrafts, ceramics, handmade goods
  • Thai cosmetics — THANN, Karmakamet (premium Thai brands, good as gifts)
  • Thai silk scarves & accessories — SookSiam vendors
  • Branded goods if on your list — prices are comparable to duty-free
🚈 → ⛵ BTS + Free shuttle boat Nana → Saphan Taksin BTS → Sathorn Pier → ICONSIAM boat · ~35 min BTS ~40 THB · boat free
Fountain show tip: Watch from the riverside terrace outside (free) not from inside the mall. Get there by 6:45 PM for the 7 PM show — it fills up fast. Bring her here for the 7 PM show — it's genuinely spectacular and unexpected for a mall.

🚈 BTS Skytrain Nana → Mo Chit (direct, end of Sukhumvit Line) · ~30 min ~44 THB each way
9:00 AM
🛍 Chatuchak Weekend Market — world's largest weekend market
Free entrySat–Sun 9 AM – 6 PM

Over 15,000 stalls across 27 sections covering 35 acres. You need 3–4 hours minimum. Go early — before 11 AM the heat is manageable and crowds are lighter.

Section guide (suggested route):
• Section 2–4: Women's clothing — the bulk of trendy fashion. This is where your girlfriend will spend most time. Prices: 150–400 THB per item. Negotiate everything.
• Section 7: Art, paintings, ceramics — unique pieces, local artists.
• Section 10–12: Home decor, wooden handicrafts, antiques. Good for unique souvenirs.
• Section 14–16: Young fashion, streetwear, accessories.
• Section 24–25: Food section. Stop here around 11–12 PM. Coconut ice cream in the coconut shell (70 THB) is unmissable.

The market has info counters, ATMs, and toilets inside. Bring enough cash — most stalls are cash only. Carry 3,000–4,000 THB in cash between you.

What to buy at Chatuchak — the best options
  • Handmade Thai jewellery — silver rings, resin pieces, unique designs (Sec 26)
  • Vintage & retro fashion — Sec 2–4, excellent finds for 150–300 THB
  • Dresses, skirts, two-piece sets — fashionable, affordable, Thai-sized cuts
  • Wooden keychains, carved figurines — unique Thai handicrafts
  • Thai ceramics & pottery — good quality, packable sizes
  • Art prints & paintings — affordable local art (Sec 7)
  • Elephant pants & linen wear — iconic Thai market pieces
  • Dried herbs & spice packets — great for taking home (curry pastes, etc.)
Chatuchak tips: Download the Chatuchak map app (or find a printed map at the entrance info counter). It's easy to get lost — the map shows section numbers. Keep hydrated — buy coconut water from vendors every hour. If you see something you like, buy it there — you won't find the same stall again.
12:30 PM
🌴 Lunch + escape the heat — Siam area
150–250 THB/person

Leave Chatuchak by 1 PM latest. Take BTS from Mo Chit back south to Siam station (~10 min, ~25 THB). Siam has major air-conditioned malls — perfect for the hottest part of the day.

Siam Paragon food court (basement) — good quality Thai food, 100–200 THB per dish. Or CentralWorld food court — larger variety, same prices. Both are within 2 min walk of Siam BTS.

After lunch, browse these malls for 30–45 min. Good for last-minute gifts:

• Siam Paragon — premium Thai brands (Naraya bags, Jim Thompson silk), beauty, food hall.
• CentralWorld — wider price range, all brands, huge food hall.
• MBK Center (short BTS or walk) — phone accessories, budget fashion, souvenirs.

🚈 BTS Mo Chit → Siam (direct, ~10 min) ~25 THB
Last-minute shopping at Siam
  • Naraya bags (Siam Paragon) — Thai brand, quality fabric bags, 300–800 THB, great gifts
  • Jim Thompson silk — premium Thai silk products, souvenirs
  • Thai snack packs — airport prices are higher, buy here
  • Cosmetics — Siam Paragon beauty hall has good Thai brands cheaper than airport

Practical

Tips, rules & things to know

🌡
Beat the heat
Outdoor activities only before 11 AM and after 5 PM. During 12–4 PM: malls, hotel, or air-con cafes. Drink water constantly — a bottle per hour outdoors minimum. 7-Eleven is everywhere.
💳
Money
Get a Rabbit Card from any BTS station (~100 THB deposit). Works on BTS and MRT. Load 600 THB between you to start. Markets are cash only — always carry 1,000–2,000 THB in small bills.
👗
Temple dress code
Grand Palace is strictest — covered shoulders AND knees. No shorts, no sleeveless. Sarongs to borrow at the gate, but they run out. Wear a light linen shirt. Remove shoes before entering halls.
🚗
Getting around
BTS + MRT for 90% of the trip. Grab for airport and any destination not near a station. Avoid metered taxis that won't use the meter. Never take stationary tuk-tuks — they will take you to gem shops.
☂️
Rain prep
40% rain chance on Thu and Sat. Pack a compact umbrella — doubles as sun shade. Rain in Bangkok is usually short and heavy — if it starts, step into a 7-Eleven or covered walkway for 15 min. It passes.
📱
Apps to install
Grab (transport), Google Maps with Bangkok downloaded offline, Line (contact salons/book appointments), Google Translate camera mode (for Thai menus).
🛡
Scam awareness
Common scam: someone tells you the Grand Palace is "closed today" and offers an alternative tour. It's never closed for this reason. Walk straight to the ticket window. Also: tuk-tuk "sightseeing tours" that end at gem shops.
🇹🇭
Basic Thai courtesy
Sawasdee krap/ka (hello), khob khun krap/ka (thank you), thao rai (how much). Always smile — it opens doors. Never raise your voice. A calm smile gets better service and better prices.
🏨
Hotel tips
Ask lyf Sukhumvit 8 for early check-in at booking. They often accommodate it free. Store bags on checkout day (Day 3) — standard free service. Use the rooftop terrace in the evenings for a quiet wind-down.
